The prime rate hit 18 1/2 percent at most major banks, with Chase Manhattan leading the move from 17 3/4. The new level, the highest since last May, signals higher general interest rates, and in New York, where usury ceilings have just been lifted, it could mean higher consumer loan charges. (Page D1.)
 
 Water pollution standards can be enforced regardless of whether an individual factory can afford compliance, the Supreme Court ruled in a major victory for the Environmental Protection Agency. The 8-to-0 decision reversed an appeals court. (A21.)
 
 Companies
 
 Agribusiness and chemical companies are shaping research at the University of California, an array of critics contends. They cite the influence of corporate gifts and grants as well as charging that university officials, among t hem two associates of President-elect Ronald Reagan , approved funds for agricultural research beneficial tocompanies in which they had a financial interest. (D1.)
 
 United States Steel will sell to Sohio coal mines and reserves in a $750 million transaction. The sale, which signals a major thrust by Sohio to serve utilities converting to coal, still leaves U.S. Steel a 300-year supply of coal, analysts said. (D1.)
 
 Bechtel announced a major reorganization in which a new holding company will be created to oversee three operating companies, each focusing on a major industry. S.D. Bechtel Jr. becomes chairman of the holding company; George P. Shultz, the Reagan adviser and former Treasury Secretary, becomes president. (D4.)
 
 Honeywell announced its long-awaited entry into the market for super minicomputers, and presented a host of new products for the automated office. (D5.)
 
 ABC will introduce a cable-television network devoted to performing and visual arts in a joint venture with the Warner-Amex Satellite Entertainment Company. Underwriters will be sought for the so-called Alpha service, which will be delivered free to cable subscribers.  (C30.)
 
 American Invsco agreed to turn over financial records it had been withholding from a House subcommittee in a last-minute deal that averted a contempt-of-Congress charge. The material had been subpoenaed as part of a Congressional investigation of national condominium sales practices. (D5.) International
 
 Authority to negotiate curbs on Japanese car imports was approved for the President in a 317-to-57 House vote.  Under the resolution, which was strongly backed by the United Autom obile Workers and Ford Motor, the negotiating power will expire in t wo and a half years. Themeasure's prospects in the Senate are uncerta in. (D1.)
 
 Europe's exchange rate system will be extended for two years, heads of government agreed after a two-day meeting. The Common Market countries also presented a plan to encourage wider commercial use of their new monetary unit, known as the Ecu, in private capital markets. (D1.)
 
 Reporting about Haiti's explosive economic problems appears to underlie the Government's recent crackdown on journalists. This Friday Haiti will hold a crucial meeting with the International Monetary Fund to negotiate a $44 million credit to ease its desperate foreign-exchange shortage. In the year-long discussions, Haiti has been unable to meet the fund's requirements. (A3.) Markets
 
 Stock prices closed mostly down because of the prime rate jump, but the Dow Jones industrial average rose 4.95 points, to 974.40, in a final-hour gain led by computer and energy issues. (D8.)
 
 The Federal Reserve temporarily sold securities, draining funds from the banking system, and thus served notice that it does not intend to let short-term interest rates fall; the result was a further rise in such rates yesterday. (D7.) The dollar gained against most major currencies, fueled by the prime rate rise; gold prices fell. (D11.) Metals futures were also swept lower. (D11.) Today's Columns
 
 Controlling inflation via the money supply is an ideal inevitably undercut by national budget deficits, a way of life in a democratic society. Even a Constitutional amendment banning deficits may only stimulate off-budget financing, as the Government continues to respond to private interests. Leonard Silk. Economic Scene. (D2.)
 
 GK Technologies is now considered a bargain stock after the issue plummeted 10 7/8 points last week, when a merger with the Penn Central Corporation was called off. Market Place. (D8.)
